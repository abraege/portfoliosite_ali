Kayapp (case study)

Overview

Start your next adventure with Kayapp, your river adventure companion.

Outdoor athletes must navigate a constantly changing landscape. Kayapp is a responsive web application that provides river sport athletes with essential condition reports and need-to-know details for trip planning. In our river community, users ask questions, follow their friends and favorite athletes, share their trip journals, and mark new hazards. Users plan better, safer trips, and most importantly, have more fun on the water.

Role

This project brief required designing a water and weather-related responsive web-app that provided current and predicted condition reports. The project duration was 6 months and followed a human-centered design model. The design process required researching the target demographic and creating a clean, intuitive design that portrays high-level data that is easy to understand.

Discovery

The discovery process began with a competitive analysis on other online resources and trip planning apps on the market. I interviewed potential users and found that certain weather metrics were universally preferred for “at-a-glance” while other metrics were more niche. Social features were desired for pre-trip features rather than past tense trip logging. Satellite and offline modes were considered critical for real-time weather alerts in the backcountry.

Friction points were the need for: expensive equipment, permitting weather, outdoor common sense, and, oftentimes, an experienced friend. I asked,How might we reduce the barriers to outdoor recreation?

Ideation

I created user personas whose needs mirrored those of my (earlier) interviewed potential users. The personas provided me with the user’s “why:”

  • Gaining confidence in river knowledge and condition reports
  • Connecting with other athletes
  • Planning trips

Essential Flows

My flows highlighted three key functions informed by the initial user interviews, personas, and user journeys:

  1. Sharing rapid descriptions from the “maps” page
  2. Creating a weather alert
  3. Editing your profile

Key Improvements

Users performed three moderated user testing tasks related to the web-app’s key functions. I found and resolved several errors with button placement and icon choice, and information availability.

UX Goals

  • Display most relevant data first, in easy-to-read formats
  • Allow interactive map elements and exploration
  • Simplify hazard alerts while retaining specificity and “niche” settings
  • Integrate social features and user-generated data to connect river community
